Petal Search — Huawei RU

Product design on Huawei's Petal Search inside the Russian branch — building a default search experience on Android in a market with strong incumbents and tight platform constraints.

// BRIEF

Petal Search is Huawei's first-party search engine. In Russia it had to compete with deeply entrenched defaults and adapt to local content sources, behaviors, and language — all while staying coherent with global design guidelines.

I worked as a product designer in the Russian branch, owning search UX on mobile and feeding fixes back into the global codebase.

Platform
Android, HarmonyOS
Locale
RU (primary)
Stage
Live · iterating
Surfaces
Search, results, browser

// APPROACH

The interesting part wasn't the visuals — it was the local content surface. People search differently here: news-heavy, maps-heavy, image-heavy. We restructured the SERP around what users actually wanted, not what shipped in the global template.

  • Ran content-type audits on real Russian search logs and mapped them to result-card variants.
  • Designed local rich-result formats — news, maps, image carousels — fitting the global system.
  • Worked with global team on dark mode, typography scaling for Cyrillic, and accessibility passes.
  • Shipped onboarding tweaks that bumped retention on first-time installs.
